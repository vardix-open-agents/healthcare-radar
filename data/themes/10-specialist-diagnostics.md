# Theme: Specialist Diagnostics & AI Imaging

## Summary
AI-powered diagnostic tools for specific specialties - radiology, dermatology, oncology, neurology. These assist specialists in diagnosis and analysis.

## Companies
- **Dermicus** (Sweden) - Teledermatology platform. GPs send images to dermatologists for remote consultation.
- **Neko Health** (Sweden) - AI-powered body scanning. Founded by Daniel Ek (Spotify). 3 clinics in Sweden. Full-body scanning in 30 minutes.
- **Radiobotics** (Denmark) - AI X-ray analysis for MSK conditions. CE marked. RBknee product.
- **Walr** (Sweden) - Dermatology AI for skin condition identification from photos.
- **TytoCare** (Israel/EU) - Remote examination devices (stethoscope, otoscope). $250M+ funding. CE marked.
- **Docrates** (Sweden) - Cancer second opinion service. Network of oncologists.

## Growth Signal
**MEDIUM** - Specialized diagnostics is high-value but niche. Requires specialist adoption. Hardware plays (TytoCare, Neko) have different economics.

## Evidence
- TytoCare: $250M+ funding, CE marked, NHS deployments
- Neko Health: Series A, Daniel Ek backing, 3 clinics
- Radiobotics: CE marked, Danish hospital deployments
- Multiple Swedish dermatology plays

## Drivers
- Radiologist/specialist shortages
- AI maturity for image analysis
- Specialist wait times
- Early detection value
- Remote diagnostics demand
- Premium consumer health market (Neko)

## Varden Fit
**LOW-MODERATE** - Specialist diagnostics is downstream from Varden's primary care marketplace. However:

- **Dermicus** - Could integrate with Varden's referral workflow. GPs on Varden could use Dermicus for dermatology consults.
- **Walr** - Dermatology AI could be part of Varden's triage ("Upload a photo for AI analysis before booking").
- **Docrates** - Cancer second opinions could be offered through Varden's specialist network.

But these are specialized features, not core infrastructure.

## Opportunity
1. **Dermatology triage integration** - Walr or Dermicus could enhance Varden's symptom checker for skin conditions. "Upload photo → AI suggests condition → Book appropriate provider."

2. **Specialist referral tools** - Dermicus model (GP to specialist consult) could be generalized to other specialties on Varden.

3. **Premium diagnostics partnerships** - Neko Health shows premium preventive screening demand. Varden could partner to offer through marketplace.

4. **Don't build diagnostics AI** - Radiobotics and similar are deep specialist tools. Not Varden's competency.

**Specialist diagnostics is interesting for partnership but not build. Focus on referral workflow integration.**
