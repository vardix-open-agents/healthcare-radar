# Theme: Telemedicine Platforms

## Summary
Comprehensive platforms enabling remote healthcare delivery through video consultations, digital prescriptions, and virtual care workflows.

## Companies
- **Kry/Livi** (Sweden) - Europe's leading digital healthcare provider. 200M+ patient interactions. $700M+ funding. $2B+ valuation. Operating in Sweden, Norway, UK, France, Germany.
- **Min Doktor** (Sweden) - 24/7 online consultations. Partnership with Apotek Hjärtat for vaccinations. Multiple clinician types (doctors, nurses, psychologists).
- **Doktor.se** (Sweden) - Hybrid model with digital platform + physical health centers. €29.5M from Tencent. Also powers veterinary care (AniCura).
- **Vidhing** (Sweden) - Healthcare-specific video consultation platform with EHR integration.
- **DoctorOnNet** (Sweden) - Online doctor consultations and prescriptions.
- **Zava** (UK/EU) - Online doctor and pharmacy. 5M+ patients. Operating in UK, Germany, France, Italy.
- **Zando Health** (Germany) - Digital prescription platform.

## Growth Signal
**HIGH** - Kry/Livi's scale ($2B+ valuation) proves the telemedicine model. COVID accelerated adoption. Post-COVID, hybrid care is the norm.

## Evidence
- Kry: $700M+ funding, $2B+ valuation, 200M+ interactions
- Doktor.se: €29.5M from Tencent
- Zava: 5M+ patients
- Min Doktor: Apotek Hjärtat partnership
- Multiple well-funded players = validated market

## Drivers
- COVID normalization of video consultations
- Patient convenience expectations
- Provider efficiency (see more patients remotely)
- Geographic barriers to care
- Cost reduction potential for payers

## Varden Fit
**MODERATE-HIGH** - Telemedicine is complementary but potentially competitive:
- **Complementary**: Varden's clinic partners could offer video consults through Varden's platform
- **Competitive**: Kry, Min Doktor, Doktor.se compete for the same patients

The question: Should Varden build telemedicine features, or partner with telemedicine providers?

## Opportunity
1. **Partner rather than compete** - Add Kry/Min Doktor/Doktor.se as providers on Varden's marketplace. When patients search for care, telemedicine options appear alongside in-person providers.

2. **White-label Vidhing** - Vidhing is infrastructure, not D2C. Varden could white-label their video platform to offer telemedicine capabilities to Varden's clinic partners.

3. **Hybrid care coordination** - Help patients navigate between telemedicine and in-person care. If Kry can't help, Varden routes to appropriate specialist.

4. **Focus on physical care differentiation** - Kry/Min Doktor excel at digital. Varden's strength is the full provider network including physical clinics. Lean into that.

**Telemedicine is table stakes but not Varden's core differentiator. Partnership + infrastructure is the right approach.**
