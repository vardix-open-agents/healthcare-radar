# Theme: Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM)

## Summary
Platforms that enable continuous monitoring of patients at home through connected devices, IoT sensors, and digital health tracking.

## Companies
- **Dignio** (Norway) - Connected care platform for chronic disease and elderly care. Deployed across Norwegian municipalities. UK and European expansion.
- **Doccla** (UK) - Virtual ward RPM for NHS. IoT devices + clinical dashboards. Multiple conditions.
- **Monsenso** (Denmark) - Mental health monitoring with behavioral data and digital biomarkers. Funded by Innovation Fund Denmark.
- **Lapsi** (Finland) - Child health tracking, vaccination schedules, development milestones.
- **Natural Cycles** (Sweden) - Fertility tracking and digital birth control. FDA-cleared. €50.8M Series C.

## Growth Signal
**MEDIUM-HIGH** - Virtual wards accelerated by COVID. Chronic disease management is a growing need with aging population. But requires hospital/municipality sales cycles, not clinic-focused.

## Evidence
- Dignio: Deployed across Norwegian municipalities
- Doccla: Multiple NHS trust deployments
- Natural Cycles: €50.8M funding, FDA clearance
- Monsenso: Innovation Fund Denmark grant
- Growing policy focus on "hospital at home"

## Drivers
- Aging population with chronic conditions
- Hospital capacity constraints
- COVID proving home monitoring works
- IoT device commoditization
- Policy shift toward preventive/community care
- Cost savings from reduced hospitalizations

## Varden Fit
**LOW-MODERATE** - RPM is hospital/municipality focused, not primary care clinic. Varden's clinic network is private primary care/specialists, not chronic disease management programs.

However, there's an adjacency:
- Post-discharge monitoring could be offered through Varden clinics
- Preventive monitoring for at-risk patients
- Integration with specialist providers on Varden

## Opportunity
1. **Specialist integration** - Some Varden specialists (cardiologists, endocrinologists) could benefit from RPM for their chronic patients. Partner with Dignio/Doccla to offer through Varden specialists.

2. **Preventive health tracking** - Natural Cycles and Lapsi show consumer demand for health tracking. Could Varden offer general health tracking to patients using its marketplace?

3. **Municipality partnerships** - If Varden wants to serve public sector, RPM could be an entry point. But this is a different sales motion.

4. **Observe, don't build** - RPM requires hardware, clinical protocols, and long sales cycles. Not core to Varden's marketplace model.

**RPM is interesting but not core. Monitor for partnership opportunities with specialists, but don't prioritize building.**
