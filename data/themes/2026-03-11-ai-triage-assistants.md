# Theme Report: AI Triage Assistants

**Date:** 2026-03-11
**Status:** Early detection — 4 entries clustered
**Growth Trajectory:** High

---

## Summary

AI-powered triage is emerging across multiple care settings — from emergency dispatch to primary care intake. The core pattern: real-time symptom analysis → urgency classification → routing recommendation.

## Evidence

| Company | Country | Setting | Traction |
|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| Corti | Denmark | Emergency dispatch | $60M funding, EU deployments |
| Infermedica | Poland | Primary care intake | 10M+ users, API-first |
| Ada | Germany | Symptom checker app | Consumer app, B2B pilots |
| NHS pilots | UK | 111 helpline | Government-backed trials |

## Key Technologies

- NLP / LLMs for symptom interpretation
- Speech recognition (voice-based triage)
- Structured medical knowledge bases
- Real-time decision support

## Drivers

- **Provider burnout** — Reduce cognitive load on frontline staff
- **Patient access** — Faster routing to appropriate care
- **Cost pressure** — Avoid unnecessary ER visits

## Opportunity for Varden

**AI Intake + Booking Router**

- Patient describes symptoms (chat or voice)
- AI triages urgency + care type
- Auto-matches to available provider
- Books appointment in one flow

**Why it fits:**
- Leverages our EHR integrations (booking)
- Serves existing clinics (admin efficiency)
- Infrastructure play (scales across all providers)
- Low regulatory risk (routing, not diagnosis)

**Risk:** Clinical triage has liability implications. Keep it "care navigation" not "medical advice."

## Related Opportunities

1. **Post-booking prep** — AI-generated intake forms pre-populated from triage
2. **Provider matching** — Triage → recommend specific provider based on specialty + availability
3. **Follow-up routing** — After visit, auto-suggest next step (specialist, lab, imaging)
